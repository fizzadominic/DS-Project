An ordered set of numbers, formed according to a definite members are called terms (or elements) of the sequence. rule, is called a sequence (or progression) and the individual A common representation of a finite sequence, one that has a finite number n of terms, is

a 1, a2, a3,..., an

or

a (k = 1, 2, 3,...,n),

which is read "a, where k equals from 1 to n". If the sequence has an unlimited number of terms, thus called an infinite sequence, we may write

a 1, a2, a3....

or

a (k = 1, 2, 3,...).

We will denote the nth term of a sequence by T (ort). Then T1, T2, T3....(t1, t2, t3...) will denote respectively the first, second, third, ... terms of the sequence.

The suffix n in Tn (or tn) indicates the position or rank of the term in the sequence.
